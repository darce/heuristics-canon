# Licensing and rights

One page: what you may reuse, what is not ours to license, and why some material is not
published at all.

## What you may reuse

Everything published in this repository is licensed **CC BY 4.0** ([`LICENSE`](LICENSE)):
the rules, PRINCIPLES.md, SOURCES.md, the release manifest, and the prose around them.
Share and adapt for any purpose, including commercially, with credit. Attribution string:

> Heuristics Canon by Daniel Arcé (github.com/darce/heuristics-canon), licensed under CC BY 4.0.

## What is not ours to license

Every rule cites a third-party source, and a citation is not a license: CC BY 4.0 grants
no rights in any cited work. The books, papers, and standards named in
[`SOURCES.md`](SOURCES.md) belong to their authors and publishers.

Every cited work is used `research-copy-only` — cited, never reproduced. Some sources
carry their own permissive terms (for example W3C standards and CC BY 4.0 material), but
this repository reproduces none of their text either way, so the single conservative
posture covers all of them. If you are a rights-holder and believe a rule or citation
overreaches, open an issue.

## Why some material is not published

Copyright protects a source's expression, not its ideas. A one-line falsifiable rule we
authored is our own expression and safe to publish; a chapter-by-chapter condensation of
a book can substitute for the book and is not. So this repository publishes the rules
and no distillation files at all. The condensations, extracted text, and page notes live
in a private research corpus and are not distributed.
